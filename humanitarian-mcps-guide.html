What exactly is an MCP?

MCP stands for Model Context Protocol — think of it as a plug-in that lets an AI assistant reach out to a live database and fetch real facts on your behalf.

  • 1
    You ask a question "How many people were displaced in Sudan last year?"
  • 2
    The AI decides which tool to use It recognises this needs the UNHCR Refugee Statistics MCP.
  • 3
    The MCP fetches live data It queries the real UNHCR database — not old training data.
  • 4
    You get a factual, sourced answer Numbers come directly from official humanitarian databases.
The Six Tools

Meet the MCPs

Each tool connects to a different official humanitarian database. Together they cover disaster response, food security, famine early warning, displacement, and refugee resettlement.

IFRC GO

International Red Cross & Red Crescent

Real-time disaster alerts, emergency operations, deployed teams, and field reports from the world's largest humanitarian network.

HDX

Humanitarian Data Exchange

Open data on food prices, food security, population counts, funding, conflict events, and more — across dozens of countries.

UNHCR Refugee Statistics

UN Refugee Agency — Population Data

Official global statistics on refugees, asylum seekers, internally displaced people, and stateless persons by country and year.

UNHCR Resettlement

UN Resettlement Data (RSQ)

Submissions, departures, demographics, and destination country data for refugees going through formal resettlement programmes.

FEWS NET

Famine Early Warning Systems Network

Early warning intelligence on food insecurity — market prices, crop yields, trade flows, rainfall, labour data, IPC classifications, and scenario forecasts for crisis-prone countries.

IPC-CH

Integrated Food Security Phase Classification

The global standard for measuring acute food insecurity and acute malnutrition severity. Provides IPC/CH phase maps, population counts by phase, and tracking data across countries and time periods.

Deep Dives

What can each MCP actually do?

Let's walk through each one with plain-English examples.

IFRC GO

Disaster response at your fingertips

IFRC GO is the platform used by Red Cross / Red Crescent societies to coordinate disaster operations globally. The MCP gives you live access to events, surge alerts, deployed emergency teams (ERUs), field reports, and country-level disaster histories.

🚨 Live Events Current disasters and crises being tracked worldwide
📋 Field Reports Ground-level situation reports from affected areas
🏕️ Emergency Teams Which ERUs are deployed and where
📢 Surge Alerts Urgent requests for specialist volunteers
💰 Appeals Funding appeals and financial coverage data
Country Profiles Disaster history and NS capacity by country
💬 Example conversation
You: "What active disaster operations is the Red Cross running right now in East Africa?"
AI (using IFRC GO MCP):
"There are currently 3 active IFRC operations in East Africa: a Drought response in Somalia (appeal target $4.2M, 58% funded), Flood response in Kenya's Rift Valley (field report filed 2 days ago), and a disease outbreak response in Ethiopia. Two Emergency Response Units — a Water & Sanitation ERU and a Health ERU — are deployed to the region. Would you like the full field report for any of these?"
💬 Another example
You: "Show me all surge alerts in the past 30 days"
AI: Fetches the latest surge alerts from IFRC GO, showing position name, required skills, country, and open/closed status — so coordinators can spot where specialists are still needed.
HDX

Numbers that explain a crisis

The Humanitarian Data Exchange is OCHA's open data platform. The MCP unlocks food prices, food security levels (IPC phases), population counts, conflict event data, rainfall, funding flows, and operational presence of NGOs — all filterable by country, admin level, and date range.

🍞 Food Prices Market prices for staple foods (WFP data)
🚨 Food Security IPC/CH severity phases by region
👥 Population Estimated population by admin area
💥 Conflict Events ACLED-sourced armed conflict incidents
🌧️ Rainfall CHIRPS rainfall anomaly data
💵 Funding FTS humanitarian funding by country
🏢 NGO Presence Which organisations are operating where
📉 Poverty Rates Multidimensional poverty indicators
💬 Example conversation
You: "How has the price of sorghum changed in Khartoum markets over the past year?"
AI (using HDX MCP):
"WFP market price data for Khartoum shows sorghum averaged SDG 980/kg in June 2024, rising 140% to SDG 2,350/kg by May 2025, driven by supply chain disruptions from the ongoing conflict. Prices peaked in November 2024. Compared to the 5-year average, current prices are 3.2× higher — a significant food security risk."
💬 Another example
You: "Which provinces in DRC are in IPC Phase 3 or worse right now?"
AI: Queries HDX food security data for the DRC and returns a breakdown of admin1 regions by IPC phase, population affected at each severity level, and a note on the data date — giving a fast snapshot of where acute hunger is concentrated.
UNHCR Refugee Statistics

The definitive global displacement picture

UNHCR is the UN's refugee agency. This MCP gives you access to their official global statistics database — covering refugees, asylum seekers, internally displaced persons (IDPs), returnees, and stateless people. You can filter by origin country, host country, and year going back decades.

Population Totals Refugees, IDPs, asylum seekers by country & year
📝 Asylum Applications New applications filed in any country
⚖️ Asylum Decisions Recognition rates, rejections, appeal outcomes
👨‍👩‍👧 Demographics Age and gender breakdowns of displaced populations
🏠 Solutions Voluntary returns, resettlement, naturalisations
📊 Trends Over Time Multi-year comparisons for any country/group
💬 Example conversation
You: "Which country hosts the most refugees right now, and where do those refugees come from?"
AI (using UNHCR Refugee Statistics MCP):
"As of the latest UNHCR data, Iran hosts approximately 3.8 million refugees — the largest total globally. The vast majority (around 3.4 million) are Afghan nationals who fled decades of conflict. Germany and Pakistan follow as the second and third largest host countries. Would you like a demographic breakdown or historical trend?"
💬 Another example
You: "What's the asylum recognition rate for Syrians applying in Germany over the last 5 years?"
AI: Queries asylum decision data filtered to Syrian applicants in Germany across years, then calculates and presents the recognition rate (positive decisions / total decisions) with year-by-year trends — useful for legal aid organisations and journalists.
UNHCR Resettlement (RSQ)

The pathway from camp to new home

Resettlement is a formal process where a refugee is moved from their first country of asylum to a third country that agrees to grant them permanent residence. The RSQ (Resettlement Statistical Query) MCP tracks submissions (referrals), actual departures, destination countries, and demographics of resettled refugees.

📤 Submissions UNHCR referrals submitted to resettlement countries
✈️ Departures Refugees who actually departed for resettlement
🏳️ Destinations Which countries accept the most resettled refugees
🌐 Origins Nationality of refugees being resettled
📁 Categories Resettlement need categories (protection, medical…)
👤 Demographics Age/gender of resettled population
💬 Example conversation
You: "How many Somali refugees were resettled to the US in 2023, and how does that compare to 2019?"
AI (using UNHCR Resettlement MCP):
"In 2023, 8,420 Somali refugees departed for resettlement to the United States. In 2019 the figure was 2,190 — so the 2023 number represents a 285% increase. The rise reflects the US raising its resettlement ceiling from 18,000 to 125,000 between those years. Would you like a comparison with other destination countries?"
💬 Another example
You: "Which countries have the largest gap between UNHCR submissions and actual departures?"
AI: Compares submissions vs departures data by destination country, calculates the percentage gap, and surfaces which countries are accepting far fewer people than UNHCR has referred — a critical advocacy metric.
FEWS NET

The famine early warning system

FEWS NET (Famine Early Warning Systems Network) is a USAID-funded programme that monitors food security in the world's most crisis-prone countries. It goes far beyond a single indicator — it integrates market prices, crop production, rainfall, trade flows, labour wages, displacement, nutrition, and IPC classifications into a single coherent early warning picture. Think of it as the intelligence layer that tells you why a famine is forming before it fully arrives.

📊 IPC Classifications Phase data per geographic unit with population sizes
🌾 Crop Production Harvest estimates, yield forecasts, and seasonal data
🛒 Market Prices Staple food prices at specific markets over time
🌧️ Rainfall Estimates CHIRPS dekadal rainfall vs. long-term average
🚛 Trade Flows Cross-border commodity movements and access
👷 Labour & Wages Days of work available, casual labour wage rates
💸 Remittances Household income from remittances by country
🏕️ Displacement Tracking IOM displacement figures integrated with food security
📝 Scenario Forecasts Most-likely and worst-case food security outlooks
🌡️ Heat Exposure Heat stress data linked to agricultural livelihoods
🩺 Nutrition GAM/SAM rates and nutrition indicator values
📄 Narratives & Reports Analyst-written situation reports and assumption sets
💬 Example conversation
You: "Why are food prices so high in southern Madagascar right now? Give me the full picture."
AI (using FEWS NET MCP):
"FEWS NET data points to three converging drivers in southern Madagascar: (1) Rainfall was 42% below the long-term average in the last dekad — among the lowest on record for this season. (2) Maize market prices in Ambovombe are currently 2.8× the 5-year average, reflecting supply shortfalls from two consecutive poor harvests. (3) Trade route access data shows the RN13 road has been impassable for 6 weeks due to cyclone damage, cutting off the main supply corridor. The current FEWS NET outlook classifies the region at IPC Phase 3–4 through at least Q3. The worst-case scenario projects localised Phase 5 (Catastrophe) if the next growing season also fails."
💬 Another example
You: "Show me crop yield forecasts for Ethiopia's Belg season compared to last year"
AI: Queries FEWS NET crop yield forecast indicators for Ethiopia filtered to the Belg season, compares current-year forecasts against prior-year actuals by region, and flags areas where yield is projected to fall more than 25% — a key threshold for food security deterioration.
IPC-CH

The global hunger severity scale

The IPC (Integrated Food Security Phase Classification) is the universally accepted framework for measuring how severe hunger is in a population. It classifies food security on a 5-phase scale — from Phase 1 (Minimal) to Phase 5 (Catastrophe/Famine). The IPC-CH MCP gives direct access to the official IPC database: analyses by country, geographic breakdowns, population counts at each phase, and tracking over time. CH (Cadre Harmonisé) is the equivalent system used in West and Central Africa.

📋 IPC Analyses List all official IPC analyses by country and date
🗺️ Phase Maps Geographic unit classifications with phase levels
👥 Population by Phase How many people are in each IPC phase, where
📍 Points & Areas Precise geographic area and point-level data
📈 Population Tracking Time-series: how populations move between phases
Country Totals National-level population in acute food insecurity
💬 Example conversation
You: "How many people in Nigeria are currently in IPC Phase 3 or above, and how has that number changed over the past two years?"
AI (using IPC-CH MCP):
"According to the latest IPC analysis for Nigeria, approximately 26.5 million people are in acute food insecurity (IPC Phase 3 or above) in the current period. The population tracking data shows this has risen from 19.4 million two years ago — a 37% increase. The northeast states of Borno, Yobe, and Adamawa account for the majority of Phase 4 (Emergency) cases, driven by ongoing conflict disrupting livelihoods and markets. Would you like a breakdown by state or a comparison with other West African countries?"
💬 Another example
You: "Which countries currently have populations in IPC Phase 5 (Famine/Catastrophe)?"
AI: Queries IPC analyses across all countries, filters to those with any population classified at Phase 5, and returns a ranked list with population figures — giving a fast global picture of the most extreme hunger situations currently documented.

Beyond Claude

Using these MCPs with other AI assistants

MCP is an open standard. Any AI assistant that supports it can connect to these humanitarian databases — not just Claude. Here's what that looks like in practice.

OpenAI GPT

GPT-4o and o3 models support tool calling. By wrapping the IFRC / HDX / UNHCR APIs in custom functions or through MCP bridges, you can build GPT-powered chatbots for humanitarian dashboards.

  • Embed in a Slack bot for a field office
  • Power a custom GPT that briefings team on new crises
  • Drive an automated sitrep generator
Microsoft Copilot

Microsoft 365 Copilot integrates with Power Automate and custom connectors. Humanitarian orgs using SharePoint/Teams can connect HDX and UNHCR data to auto-populate Word reports or Excel dashboards.

  • Auto-fill Excel with weekly food price data
  • Generate Word sitreps from HDX in one click
  • Trigger Teams alerts on new IFRC surge calls
Google Gemini

Gemini models on Vertex AI support custom extensions and function calling. Ideal for Google Workspace-heavy organisations wanting to bring live humanitarian data into Docs and Sheets.

  • Ask Gemini in Google Docs to insert latest IDP figures
  • Build a Looker Studio dashboard fed by UNHCR data
  • Use Gemini's multimodal skills to narrate charts
Meta Llama (Open Source)

For organisations with data sensitivity requirements — like handling personal refugee case data — running Llama 3 locally with MCP connectors keeps everything on-premise while still benefiting from live humanitarian databases.

  • Air-gapped deployments for sensitive contexts
  • No data leaves the organisation's servers
  • Full control over model behaviour and updates
⚙️ Custom Agent Frameworks

Build autonomous multi-step agents using frameworks like LangChain or CrewAI. Agents can chain multiple MCPs together — e.g. detect a crisis in IFRC GO, then automatically pull HDX food security data and UNHCR displacement numbers for the same country.

  • Automated weekly crisis briefings emailed to teams
  • Real-time alerting pipelines
  • Research agents that produce full country reports
